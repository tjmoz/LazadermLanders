Frequently Asked Questions

Most dermal fillers are injectable gels that are placed beneath the skin to restore volume or enhance facial contours. This type is made from hyaluronic acid, which attracts water and gives the face structure where needed. Biostimulators like Bellafill and Sculptra trigger the body’s collagen production for long-lasting improvement.
Fillers can cause a quick pinch during injection. Several factors can reduce discomfort during your treatment:
  • Many fillers include lidocaine as part of the formulation to numb the area as we inject
  • Topical numbing cream can be used on the surface of the skin to minimize discomfort
  • Very fine needles are used during injection
  • Treatments are fast and are over in just a few minutes
Not at Lazaderm. Our approach is built around subtlety, and we are careful to choose the right products for the right areas, place them where they will do the most good and use the amount of product that will look natural in the area. We customize everything according to your facial features and personal goals.
Each product or family of fillers is different: JUVÉDERM (varies by product): 6 months to 2 years; Restylane (varies by product): 6 months to 18 months; Bellafill: Up to 5 years; Sculptra: Up to 2 years.
There is virtually no downtime with dermal fillers. You can return to work right away. However, you may notice some swelling that can give the appearance of more volume than you were expecting for the first few days. This will go down, and you will be able to see your full results.
The cost varies by product and the number of syringes used during treatment. The best way to get an accurate estimate is to schedule a free consultation at Lazaderm. Flexible financing is available through Cherry From Allè and CareCredit.
